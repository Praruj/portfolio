The person behind the models

Why I build what I build.

Before I wrote a line of Python, I spent two and a half years hosting a podcast. Not because I wanted to be on air — but because I was obsessed with understanding people. Why do they feel what they feel? What are they really saying under the surface? That obsession didn't go away when I switched to engineering. It just found a new medium.

“Data is just the world, speaking in numbers. I build systems that listen.”

At フクシマガリレイ株式会社, I work on ML pipelines, local LLM integrations, and the software that delivers these models to people who need them. The work is technical — but the goal is always the same: make sense of something complex, then do something useful with that understanding.

I hold a B.E. in Electrical & Mechanical Systems from Kyoto University of Advanced Science (2025), based in Osaka, fluent in English and Japanese. The kind of person who founded a community, hosted a podcast for years, and still ended up writing ML pipelines — because I've never been interested in just one thing.
